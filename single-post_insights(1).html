Mapping the ESG Landscape of Serbia

Sergey Chernyanskii, PhD

GEAC expert

Photo by Nikola Cirkovic

ESG agenda which has become global in nature since 2004, spreads unevenly around the world being subject to the following key factors: ·The integration of national legislation into international legal institutions through which the global ESG considerations are implemented; ·The activities of international financial institutions for which ESG considerations are part of risk management for all the financed or insured projects; ·Activities of multinationals that have already integrated ESG principles into their management systems; ·Activities of international research, educational, consulting and other NGOs that disseminate the ESG approach through their own practices; ·Own initiatives on the part of the government, public and private companies, or NGOs to introduce ESG criteria into their practices.

Serbia, according to many observers, is at the beginning of the ESG trajectory with horizons and intermediate goals coming from international agreements and conventions to which the country is already a party or plans to become that. Progressive growth in all of the above-listed positions is expected to lead to expansion of the ESG approach in land and natural resources management practices, urban planning, environmental protection activities, environmental regulation, and stakeholder engagement – all these areas can benefit from the unique experience of GEAC experts.
