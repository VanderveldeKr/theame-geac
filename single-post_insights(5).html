What’s The Difference Between Biodiversity Offsets and Other Compensatory Measures?

Sergey Chernyanskii, PhD

GEAC expert

Photo by Tania Malréchauffé

As originally conceived, biodiversity offsets are a special form of compensation for which it must be provided to quantify the outcome and compare it to the biodiversity loss that caused the offset.

To make such an approach possible, it is necessary to first develop a biodiversity metric, a system of units in which the loss of components must initially be measured and then be able to quantify the offsetting as well. The demonstrated scheme by no means exhausts the variety of offsets that have been developed worldwide to date, which can be, inter alia:

  • •Mandatory or voluntary, Financial or land-based,
  • •Prospective or retrospective,
  • •Restoring or protecting with a sub-difference of the latter into protecting and maintaining,
  • •Unilateral or aggregated,
  • •averted (or avoided) loss offsets, etc.

GEAC experts have experience in developing and optimally configuring offset strategies to achieve No Net Loss or Net Gain principles for biodiversity components, as well as in quantifying the offsetting potential of other compensations whose contribution to biodiversity conservation and restoration is uncertain and requires expert judgement.
