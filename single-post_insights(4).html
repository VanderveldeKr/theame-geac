Each Influence Has Its Own Area. How to Predict its Boundaries and Internal Structure?

Sergey Chernyanskii, PhD

GEAC expert

The concept of Area of Influence is one of the basic approaches in most existing ESG-based systems for project risk assessment and management. At the same time, it is also the most difficult to identify and often causes debates between stakeholders - the project initiator, the financing institution and third parties affected by the proposed activity.

Most ESG standards do not provide unambiguous criteria for delineating the Area of Influence, so for large projects the reasoning in favour of one or another boundary is crucial. In order to simplify and systematise the approach to defining a project's Area of Influence, the most common approach is its zoning, an example of which is illustrated in the presented diagram.

Logically, in order to identify the Area of Influence of a project or facility, an impact assessment must be completed. On the other hand, in order to plan such an assessment, it is necessary to understand in advance which area of influence of the subject of assessment might be.

GEAC experts have extensive experience in assessing impacts for various facilities and projects and can help predict the geography of relevant impacts, identify the recipients of these impacts and develop a strategy for managing these impacts within the whole Area of Influence.
