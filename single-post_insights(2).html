Habitat as a Discrete Management Unit for ESG-based Practices

Sergey Chernyanskii, PhD

GEAC expert

When assessing the risks of existing and new projects, including in ESG-based coordinate systems for a company or project, an important task is to choose a spatial unit with which to attribute both ecosystem services and impacts of the target activity. Early versions of the IFC standards proposed DMUs - Discrete Management Units - as such a unit, but this concept has since been abandoned in favour of a more ecological concept, of which DMUs are usually considered a derivative – the habitat.

It is exactly the habitat, not the ecosystem, that is considered by international financial organisations as:

  • •the basic spatial unit of biological diversity,
  • •object of classification, mapping, qualitative and quantitative assessment of condition, vulnerability ranking, impact assessment, development of measures for protection, restoration and rehabilitation.

GEAC experts have extensive experience in habitat mapping and building on this basis all the necessary programmes and strategies for a company or project, such as:

  • •Assessing impacts and risks from ongoing or proposed activities;•Assessing impacts and risks from ongoing or proposed activities;
  • •Monitoring of impacts;
  • •Assessing the quality and availability of ecosystem services, including in monetary terms;
  • •Development of biodiversity offset programmes;
  • •Designing reclamation of disturbed lands, etc.
