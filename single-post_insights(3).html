An ESG-based practice in coordinates of ecosystem services

Sergey Chernyanskii, PhD

GEAC expert

Photo by Tyler Casey

The well-known concept of ecosystem services which is understood as the totality of socially important functions of ecological systems allows us to systematise the concept of natural resource use and scale it from the global level to the level of states, administrative-territorial units, individual companies, facilities and projects.

As a derivative of ecosystems themselves, the ecosystem services are, on the one hand, a condition of any company's activities and any project, and, on the other hand, are affected by these activities. The result of this cyclical relationship demonstrates how sustainable a project, territory or state is.

GEAC experts are well-experienced in the most challenging task in relation to ecosystem services - qualitative and quantitative assessment of their availability and quality which is the key to ESG-performance assessment of any activity or territorial unit.
